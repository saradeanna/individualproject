Summary of Education

The early years in kindergarten were all about learning through play and curiosity, but they weren’t without their challenges. I remember feeling a bit scared at first because I was the youngest in my class. Before joining Q-Dees when I was 5, I had attended a Chinese kindergarten, so the transition wasn’t entirely new, but it was different. Q-Dees provided a solid foundation for developing a love for learning and building social skills.

Moving on to primary school, I started at SK Jalan 3 and was there until Year 4. It was an important period where I learned to adapt to new situations and made lifelong friends. At the end of Year 4, life took an unexpected turn when I transferred to SK Pakatan Jaya in Ipoh, Perak, to live with my grandmother. My mom was unwell, and my dad needed to be there for her, so I stayed with my grandmother during that time. Despite the challenges, I thrived academically, always striving to do my best. I was proud to maintain the top spot in my class right up until graduation. That period taught me resilience and the importance of family support.

High school was a significant chapter that pushed me further and opened my mind to new interests. From Form 1 to Form 5, I attended SMK Bandar Seri Putra (BSP), a school known for its friendly atmosphere and strong sense of community. During Form 1 to Form 3, I was part of the KAA class, where we focused on Arabic, which gave me a unique and enriching experience. It was like being in a special school within a school because we were the only KAA class. In Form 4, I took on the challenges of Additional Mathematics, Accounting, and Business, which sparked a deep interest in analytical thinking and problem-solving. It was during this time that I discovered my passion for subjects that require logical reasoning and critical thought.

My time in high school was not just about academics. I was a library prefect, and that role taught me responsibility and how to work as part of a team. Our uniforms were green, and I wore them with pride. I joined a range of extracurricular activities to broaden my horizons and pick up new skills. I started playing hockey back in primary school at SK Jalan 3 and continued in high school. Netball was another sport I loved; I joined in Form 1 and represented my school, even going on to compete at the district level. I was also active in the KAFA class, representing my school in competitions up to the Hulu Langat district, where I won first place in the syarahan contest. It was an experience that not only improved my public speaking but also boosted my confidence.

These early experiences, from kindergarten to high school, were more than just a path of academic achievement; they were about growing as a person, developing resilience, and learning to balance work and play. Each stage of my education laid the groundwork for what came next and prepared me for the challenges of higher education.
